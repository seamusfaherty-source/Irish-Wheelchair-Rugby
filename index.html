About the Sport

Wheelchair rugby is a fast-paced, full-contact sport that combines elements of basketball, handball, and rugby. Originally known as "Murderball," it is a mixed-gender Paralympic sport that emphasizes teamwork, strategy, and athleticism.

Irish Wheelchair Rugby proudly represents Ireland in international competition, bringing together athletes from across the island to compete at the highest level.

About Us

Our History

Irish Wheelchair Rugby has been growing steadily over the years, bringing together athletes from across the island to compete in one of the most exciting Paralympic sports. Our organization is dedicated to developing wheelchair rugby at all levels, from grassroots participation to elite international competition.

We are committed to providing opportunities for athletes with disabilities to excel in sport, build confidence, and represent Ireland on the world stage. Through the dedication of our players, coaches, volunteers, and supporters, we continue to grow the sport and inspire the next generation.

Our Mission

  • Promote and develop wheelchair rugby throughout Ireland
  • Provide pathways for athletes from grassroots to elite level
  • Support our clubs in delivering quality coaching and competition
  • Represent Ireland with pride in international competition
  • Foster an inclusive and supportive community for all participants

Get Involved

Want to Play Wheelchair Rugby?

Wheelchair rugby is an inclusive, mixed-gender sport that welcomes athletes with a range of physical disabilities. It is fast-paced, strategic, and incredibly fun!

Who Can Play?

Wheelchair rugby is open to athletes with disabilities that affect the function of their arms and legs, including:

  • Spinal cord injuries (Tetraplegia/Quadriplegia)
  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Muscular Dystrophy
  • Amputations (multiple limb)
  • Polio
  • Les Autres (other physical disabilities affecting multiple limbs)

Understanding the Classification System

Players are classified on a points system from 0.5 to 3.5 based on their functional ability. Teams field four players with a maximum total of 8.0 points on court at any time.

0.5

Most significant impairment affecting all four limbs. Limited trunk control and minimal arm function.

1.0-1.5

Significant impairment with limited trunk control. Some arm and hand function.

2.0-2.5

Moderate impairment with fair trunk control. Good arm function in at least one limb.

3.0-3.5

Least significant impairment. Good trunk control and arm function with minimal leg function.

Important: In the Irish League, we welcome non-classifiable players to participate and develop their skills. This provides opportunities for more people to experience the sport while developing towards potential classification.

Getting Started

  • Contact one of our clubs (see Our Clubs page)
  • Come along to a training session - no commitment required
  • Try the sport in one of our specially designed sports wheelchairs
  • Learn from experienced players and coaches
  • Progress at your own pace in a supportive environment
  • No previous wheelchair rugby experience needed!
